Blood Test

A blood test is a diagnostic tool used to analyze various components in the blood, such as red and white blood cells, platelets, glucose, electrolytes, and proteins. It can help detect health issues, monitor the effectiveness of treatments, and provide information about organ function. Blood tests are often essential for diagnosing diseases, assessing overall health, and guiding medical decisions.

Blood Temperature

Body temperature is a measure of the body's ability to generate and release heat. The average normal body temperature is about 98.6°F (37°C), but it can vary slightly depending on factors like age, activity level, and the time of day. Maintaining a stable body temperature is essential for optimal bodily functions.

Medicine Use

Medicine is used to prevent, diagnose, treat, or relieve symptoms of various health conditions. It can come in different forms—tablets, capsules, injections, creams, or liquids—and works by interacting with body systems to alleviate symptoms, fight infections, or manage chronic conditions. Safe and effective use of medicine requires following prescribed doses and understanding potential side effects.
